Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to mail the letters myself?

Yes. This is a DIY tool. You generate the letters here, then print and mail them via USPS Certified Mail. This is exactly what credit repair companies do - we just give you the letters so you can do it yourself and save $1,000+.

Why can't I just dispute online?

You can, but mailed letters are more effective. They create a legal paper trail, are harder for bureaus to auto-reject, and carry more weight under the FCRA. Professionals always use mail.

Is this legal?

Absolutely. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) gives you the legal right to dispute any information on your credit report. These letters help you exercise that right properly.

How is this different from credit repair companies?

How long does it take to see results?

How much does mailing cost?

Step 2: Identify Negative Items

Look for these types of items that may be hurting your score:

  • Collections - Unpaid debts sold to collection agencies
  • Late Payments - Any payment 30+ days past due
  • Charge-offs - Accounts written off as uncollectable
  • Hard Inquiries - Credit checks from applications
  • Public Records - Bankruptcies, judgments, liens

Step 4: How to Mail Your Letters

Why mail matters: Physical letters via Certified Mail create a legal paper trail. This is how credit repair companies do it - and now you can too.

üìã What You Need

  • Your printed dispute letter
  • Copy of your driver's license or state ID
  • Copy of a utility bill OR bank statement (proof of address)
  • A standard white envelope
  • About $5-7 for Certified Mail fees

üìÆ At the Post Office

  1. Put your letter and ID copies in the envelope
  2. Address it to the credit bureau (addresses above)
  3. Write your return address in the top left corner
  4. Tell the clerk: "I need Certified Mail with Return Receipt"
  5. Fill out the small green card they give you
  6. Pay the fee (~$5-7 total)
  7. Keep your receipt with the tracking number!

üì¨ After You Mail

  • Track delivery at USPS.com/track
  • The green card will come back signed (proof they received it)
  • Keep the green card - this is your legal proof!
  • Bureau MUST respond within 30-45 days of receipt
  • Response will come to your home address by mail

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Tips:

  • Never send originals - only copies of your ID and documents
  • Keep copies of everything - your letter, receipts, tracking info
  • One dispute per letter works best (don't cram multiple disputes)
  • Send to each bureau separately - if the item is on all 3 reports, send 3 letters
  • Don't use online disputes - letters are more effective and have legal weight

üí∞ Cost Breakdown

Each letter costs about $5-7 total to mail:

  • Certified Mail fee: ~$4.15
  • Return Receipt: ~$3.35
  • Compare to credit repair companies: $100+/month!

Step 5: Follow Up

Bureaus must respond within 30-45 days. If they don't, or if they verify the item incorrectly:

  • Send a 611 Method of Verification letter
  • File a complaint with the CFPB at consumerfinance.gov
  • Consider sending a 623 letter directly to the creditor

Your Legal Rights

Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

  • Right to dispute inaccurate information
  • Bureaus must investigate within 30 days
  • Unverified items must be removed
  • You can sue for willful violations

Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)

  • Right to request debt validation
  • Collectors must stop until they validate
  • Protection from harassment
  • Can sue collectors who violate the law
